Overview

Representations connect perception, mapping, planning, and decision making across the autonomy stack. This workshop examines how geometric, probabilistic, dense, implicit, and learned representations shape the performance, scalability, and reliability of robotic systems, from sensing and localization to navigation, manipulation, and exploration.

Bringing together researchers across SLAM, perception, planning, learning, manipulation, and autonomous navigation, the workshop will compare task-dependent trade-offs among occupancy maps, signed distance fields, Gaussian splatting, neural fields, and other representations. Its goal is to clarify practical design choices, failure modes, and open problems for robust closed-loop autonomy while bridging classical model-based and modern data-driven approaches.
